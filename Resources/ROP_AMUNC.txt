Asia-Pacific Model United Nations Conference  

General Rules of Procedure  

SECTION A: COMMITTEES  

Rule 1 Scope of these Rules of Procedure  

These Rules of Procedure shall apply throughout all meetings of all Committees at the Asia Pacific Model United Nations Conference 2007 except the Security Council, International Court of Justice and General Assembly.  

RULE 2 Regular Sessions  

There shall be eight Regular Sessions of each Committee and two sessions of the General Assembly at the Asia Pacific Model United Nations Conference.  

RULE 3 Special Sessions  

During the week of the Conference, the Secretariat, in conjunction with Committee Directors, may call one or more Special Sessions of any Committee.  

Rule 4 Agenda  

Each Committee shall discuss two issues and the order of these issues shall be decided by the Secretariat. Additional items of urgent importance may be added to the agenda of any Committee or of the General Assembly at the discretion of the Secretariat.  

Rule 5 Credentials of Committee Delegates  

All registered delegates to the Conference are deemed to possess the appropriate credentials for admission to their allocated Committee.  

Rule 6 Quorum  

Quorum shall be one half of the registered delegates of a Committee. Verification of quorum shall take place at the commencement of the first session and by delegate's request. Verification shall be by roll call.  

Rule 7 Public Observation  

No member of the public may enter the Committee Chamber without permission from the Secretariat.  

Rule 8 Statements by the Secretariat  

The Secretariat may interrupt any Committee at any time to make a statement on any subject whatsoever. 

Rule 9 Language  

The working language of the Conference shall be English.  

Rule 10 Leaving the Committee  

Delegates may leave a Committee session at any time, so long as this does not interrupt a speaker. Delegates wishing to leave a Committee session should notify the Director in advance, and should inform him or her as to their expected time of return or as to whether they will rejoin the Committee at the commencement of the next session.  

Rule 11 Note Passing  

Notes will only be passed during periods of Formal Debate or Moderated Caucus. Notes may not be frivolous or irrelevant. All notes must state clearly both to whom it is addressed and who it is from. Conference Assistants, Directors or other representatives of the Secretariat may intercept and/or read any note. No delegate except that to whom it is addressed may read a note.  

 

SECTION B: DIRECTORS  

Rule 12 Committee Directors  

Each Committee shall have two Directors.  

Rule 13 Role of Committee Directors  

The Directors of each Committee shall be responsible for facilitating debate amongst delegates, implementing and enforcing these Rules of Procedure, and communicating information to and from the Secretariat.  

Rule 14 Powers of Committee Directors  

The Directors of each Committee may open and close each session at his or her discretion; propose any motion listed under these Rules of Procedure; draw up, open and close Speakers' Lists; rule upon matters of procedure; Rules of Procedure distribute materials; censure delegates and exercise all other powers necessary to enforce these Rules of Procedure.  

Rule 15 Precedence of Delegates  

The Director shall endeavour to ensure that all delegates are accorded fair and reasonable opportunities to speak. Where appropriate, the Director may accord precedence to delegates who have not yet spoken. The Director may also accord precedence to delegates representing Member States with special or particular relationships to the issue under debate.  

Rule 16 Grounds for Censure  

The Director may censure any delegate he or she reasonably believes is knowingly acting in disregard of these rules of procedure or otherwise acting against the best interests of the Conference and Committee. Obscene, undiplomatic or frivolous behaviour may result in a warning. Directors will also censure any delegate who misrepresents or gravely distorts his or her nation's position.  

Rule 17 Effects of Censure  

Each censure shall amount to one warning. The effect of each censure shall be determined by the number of prior warnings in that session 
1) First warning No penalty; 
2 ) Second warning The delegate loses his or her speaking rights for the remainder of that session; 
3 ) Third warning The delegate shall be expelled from the Committee for the remainder of the session. If a delegate is consistently out of order across multiple sessions, and has already received two or more warnings in one session, the Secretariat may issue further sanctions.  

Rule 18 Appeals  

Exercises of the Director's discretion may be appealed. To appeal a decision, a delegate should call a Point of Order and then request an appeal. Frivolous appeals may be disallowed. The Director whose decision is under appeal shall step down for the duration of the appeal and the Committee shall be overseen by the other Director or a representative of the Secretariat. The delegate shall then speak to his or her appeal for no more than one minute. The Director may then reply in defence of his or her ruling. The appeal shall then be put to a vote. If a two-thirds majority is attained, the decision of the Director shall be over-ruled.  

 

SECTION C: ORDER OF SESSION  

Rule 19 Right to Statement  

At the beginning of each session, the Director shall invite delegates to exercise their Right to Statement. The Right to Statement allows delegates to give a short address on any topic that is not material to the substantive topic under discussion. This is at the Director's discretion.  

Rule 20 Minute of Prayer or Meditation  

At the beginning of each session, delegates may stand and request a Minute of Prayer or Meditation. The Director may ask him or her to specify a particular topic for prayer or meditation. This is at the Director's discretion.  

Rule 21 Commencement of Debate  

At the completion of any Rights to Statement or of a Minute of Prayer or Meditation, the Director shall announce the commencement of debate. If a Speaker's List was not exhausted at the completion of the previous session, that Speakers' List shall be resumed.  

Rule 22 Closure of Session  

The Director may close debate at his discretion, with due consideration applied to scheduled closing times and the facilitation of debate. Upon the closure of a session, the Director shall state the intended opening time of the next Regular Session and whether debate on the current agenda item will continue or debate on the next agenda item will commence.   

 

SECTION D CLASSES OF DEBATE  

Rule 23 Opening Statements  

Discussion of each agenda item shall begin with Opening Statements. Opening statements allow delegates to introduce their countries' positions on the issue. Each Opening Statement shall be no longer than one minute. The Director may set further time limits at his or her discretion. The Director shall recognise Member States in alphabetical order and offer each the opportunity to give an opening statement. Delegates may refrain from making an Opening Statement.  

Rule 24 Substantive Debate  

Substantive debate shall be divided into three categories 
1) Moderated Caucuses; 2 ) Un-moderated Caucuses; 3 ) Formal Debate.  

Rule 25 Procedural Debate  

Certain Procedural Motions may generate or allow for Procedural Debate. All such debate shall be in strict accordance to the rules governing Formal Debate. No speech in Procedural Debate shall be longer than one minute, and the Director may set further time limits at his or her discretion.  

 

SECTION E MODERATED CAUCUSES  

Rule 26 Initiation of Moderated Caucuses  

Moderated Caucuses must be initiated by motion. At the initiation of a Moderated Caucus, the Director shall specify to all delegates 1) The topic of the Moderated Caucus; 2 ) The duration of the Moderated Caucus; and 3 ) The time limit for individual speeches within the Moderated Caucus.  

Rule 27 Procedure in Moderated Caucuses  

Speakers shall remain at their seats within the Committee Chamber throughout the Moderated Caucus. Speakers shall stand only when recognised by the Director. No Speakers List shall be drawn up. The Director shall, at the completion of each speech, call for those wishing to speak to raise their placards and then recognise one such delegate, who shall then stand and speak. All speeches shall be made through the Director. If the Moderated Caucus was initiated by a motion from the floor, its Proposer shall have first speaking rights.  

Rule 28 Topic of Moderated Caucuses  

Moderated Caucuses shall be specifically focused upon one issue within the scope of an agenda item. If a speaker deviates from this topic, the Director may discipline him or her, or another delegate may call a Point of Order.  

Rule 29 Duration of Moderated Caucuses 

The duration of a Moderated Caucus shall be set by the Director. Delegates proposing a Moderated Caucus in a motion from the floor may propose a time limit for the Moderated Caucus. The Director may extend a Moderated Caucus by up to five minutes without a motion, but extensions of over five minutes require a Motion to Extend the Caucus to be passed. A delegate may propose a Motion to Return to Formal Debate, and if such a motion passes, the Moderated Caucus shall end.  

Rule 30 Time Limit of Speeches  

Time limits shall be strictly enforced. No speech may exceed two minutes. The Director may, at his or her discretion, apply further limits.  

 

SECTION F UNMODERATED CAUCUSES  

Rule 31 Initiation of Unmoderated Caucuses  

Unmoderated Caucuses must be initiated by motion. At the initiation of an Un-Moderated Caucus, the Director shall specify the duration of the Unmoderated Caucus to all delegates. The Director may also, at his or her discretion, specify  1) Any limits to the location of the Unmoderated Caucus; or 2 ) Whether the Speakers' List for Formal Debate remains open.  

Rule 32 Definition of Unmoderated Caucuses  

An Unmoderated Caucus is a period of discussion of a fixed length during which time delegates may circulate and discuss issues arising from the agenda item under discussion. No points or motions from the floor shall be entertained.  

Rule 33 Duration of Unmoderated Caucuses The duration of an Unmoderated Caucus shall be set by the Director. Delegates proposing an Unmoderated Caucus in a motion from the floor may propose a time limit. These time limits shall be strictly applied, but the Director may extend or call to a close Unmoderated Caucuses at his or her discretion. All delegates must return to their seats at the completion of the Unmoderated Caucus.  

Rule 34 Location of Unmoderated Caucuses  

Delegates may leave the Committee Chamber during an Unmoderated Caucus. It is their responsibility to ensure that they return to the Committee Chamber at or before the completion of the Caucus.  

Rule 35 Conduct of Directors during Unmoderated Caucus  

One Director or the Secretary shall remain within the Committee Chamber throughout the Unmoderated Caucus.  

 

SECTION G FORMAL DEBATE  

Rule 36 Primacy of Formal Debate  

Unless otherwise stated, the rules of Formal Debate are presumed to apply throughout all Committee sessions.  

Rule 37 Definition of Formal Debate  

Formal debate shall be the primary method for discussion of agenda items at the Conference. During Formal Debate, all speeches shall be made through the Director in strict accordance with the Speakers' Lists. All motions and points from the floor shall be open. Time limits for speeches shall be set by the Director, but shall be no more than five minutes.  

 

SECTION H WORKING PAPERS, DRAFT RESOLUTIONS AND AMENDMENTS  

Rule 38 Distribution of Working Papers  

Working Papers may be submitted to the Director by any delegate. To be distributed to the Committee, a Working Paper must: 1) Be signed by its Proposer; 2 ) Clearly state which Committee and agenda item it pertains to; and 3 ) Be typed and free from errors of spelling and grammar. The Director may, at his or her discretion, reject frivolous or inappropriate proposed Working Papers.  

Rule 39 Introduction of Working Papers  

Working Papers are introduced when they are distributed by the Director to all Committee delegates and may not be discussed until they are introduced. Multiple Working Papers may be discussed simultaneously and they do not generate Speakers' Lists. The Director will notify all delegates of the introduction of a Working Paper when it has been distributed to all delegates.  

Rule 40 Distribution of Draft Resolutions  

Draft Resolutions may be proposed by any Member State. A Draft Resolution must be distributed to all delegates by the Director before it may be introduced. To be distributed, a Draft Resolution must 1) Be signed by its Proposer and Seconder; 2 ) Clearly state which Committee and agenda item it pertains to; 3 ) Be signed by at least one quarter of the Member States in the Committee; 4 ) Follow correct UN Resolution format; and 5 ) Be typed and free from errors of spelling and grammar.  The Director may, at his or her discretion, reject frivolous or inappropriate Draft Resolutions.  

Rule 41 Introduction of Draft Resolutions  

Draft Resolutions are introduced by motion from the floor. If a Draft Resolution is successfully introduced 1) The Director shall close and suspend the General Speakers List; 2 ) The Proposer shall come forward to read the Operative Clauses; 3 ) Questions of Clarification shall be entertained; 4 ) Syntactic and Semantic Amendments shall be taken; 5 ) The Proposer shall speak to the Draft Resolution; 6 ) The Director shall open the Draft Resolution Speakers' List; 7) The Seconder shall speak to the Draft Resolution; then 8) The Draft Resolution Speaker's List shall commence with the first speaker against.  

Rule 42 Questions of Clarification 

 Questions of Clarification may be asked of the Proposer from the floor. Questions of Clarification may include questions about the definition of terms or the meaning of potentially ambiguous terms and phrases. Questions of Clarification may not be asked so as to induce debate on the Draft Resolution, or so as to question the Proposer's position on the agenda item.  

Rule 43 Syntactic and Semantic Amendments  

The Director or any Delegate may propose amendments to the syntax or semantics of a Draft Resolution between its introduction and the commencement of debate on it. Such amendments may be used to correct errors in spelling or punctuation, address syntactical and semantic errors, or to address clear and unintentional ambiguities within a clause. If the Proposer does not object, such amendments may be passed at the discretion of the Director.  

Rule 44 Distribution of Amendments  

Amendments may be proposed by any Member State. An Amendment must be submitted to the Director before it may be introduced. Amendments need not be distributed to all delegates, but all delegates must have the opportunity to read an amendment before any vote on its introduction. All Amendments must 1) Be signed by its Proposer and Seconder; 2 ) Clearly state which Committee, agenda item and Draft Resolution it pertains to; 3 ) Be signed by at least ten percent of the Member States in the Committee; 4 ) Follow correct UN Resolution format; and 5 ) Be typed or clearly hand-written and free from errors of spelling and grammar. The Director may, at his or her discretion, reject frivolous or inappropriate Amendments.  

Rule 45 Introduction of Amendments  

Amendments are introduced by motion from the floor. If an Amendment is successfully introduced 1) The Director shall close and suspend the Draft Resolution Speakers List; 2 ) The Proposer shall come forward an read the Amendment; 3 ) Questions of Clarification shall be entertained; 4 ) Syntactic and Semantic Amendments shall be taken; 5 ) The Committee shall carry out a vote to determine whether or not to debate the Amendment; then 6 ) If the vote to debate passes, an Amendment Speakers' List shall be opened, the Proposer shall speak to the Amendment, and the Amendment Speakers' List shall then commence with the first speaker against; or 7) If the vote to debate fails, the Amendment shall move directly to a vote.  

 

SECTION I SPEAKERS' LISTS  

Rule 46 Role of Speakers' Lists  

Speakers' Lists shall determine the speaking order throughout Formal Debate and shall be drawn up by the Director. During Formal Debate, only speakers on a Speakers' List may give speeches, although others may raise motions and points from the floor or speak if yielded to.  

Rule 47 Opening and Closure of Speakers' List  

The Director will open and close Speakers Lists by declaration. Speakers may only be added to an open Speakers' List.   

Rule 48 Addition to and Removal from the Speakers' List  

When a Speakers' List is opened, delegates may be added to the list by 1) Raising their placard if and when requested to do so by the Director; or 2 ) Sending a note to the Chair. No delegate may be added again to a Speaker's List that he or she is already on. A delegate who has spoken or been otherwise removed from a Speaker's List may be added to the Speakers' List. A delegate may be removed from the Speakers' List by sending a note to the Chair.  

Rule 49 Classes of Speakers' List  

There shall be four classes of Speakers' Lists 1) General Speakers' List; 2 ) Draft Resolution Speakers' Lists; 3 ) Amendment Speakers' Lists; 4 ) Procedural Speakers' Lists.  

Rule 50 The General Speakers' List  

The General Speakers' List shall encompass all Formal Debate on the issue between the completion of Position Speeches and commencement of debate on Draft Resolutions.  

Rule 51 Draft Resolution Speakers' Lists  

When Formal Debate commences on a Draft Resolution, the Director shall draw up and open a Speakers' List to discuss the Draft Resolution. This Speakers' List shall be arranged into speakers for and against the Draft Resolution. All speeches should substantially address the Draft Resolution. While such lists are in operation, amendments may be submitted.  

Rule 52 Proposer's Right of Reply  

At the exhaustion of a Draft Resolution Speakers' List, or otherwise immediately prior to voting on a Draft Resolution, the proposing delegate may deliver a short address summarising the advantages of the Draft Resolution. This address will be no longer than two minutes.  

Rule 53 Amendment Speakers' Lists 

 If the Committee moves to debate an amendment, the Director shall draw up an Amendment Speakers' List. This list shall function in the manner of a Draft Resolution Speakers' List, but the discussion is to be focused on the Amendment alone. No Proposer's Right of Reply shall apply.  

Rule 54 Procedural Speakers' Lists  

A Procedural Speakers' List shall be opened when debate is required on a procedural motion. All procedural motions except the Motion to Close Debate will move directly to a vote without debate unless there is an objection from the floor. Motions to Close Debate will always be debated. During a procedural debate 1) Speaking times shall be set at no more than one minute or one half of the general speaking time, whichever is the shortest; 2 ) No debate of substantive matters is to be entered into; 3 ) No yields to other delegates shall be entertained; 4 ) No more than three speakers for and against the motion shall be entertained; and 5 ) No Proposer's Right of Reply shall apply.  

Rule 55 Yielding  

At any time during substantive Formal Debate, a speaker, upon the completion of his speech, shall have the opportunity to Yield to 1) The Director, in which case the Director shall invite the next speaker forward; 2 ) Another delegate on the Speaker's List, in which case the Director shall invite that delegate forward to speak. If the original speaker did not take any Points of Information and has time remaining, the incoming speaker may speak for the sum of the time allocated to him via the Speakers' List and the remaining time on the clock for the yielding speaker. If the yielding speaker took Points of Information or exhausted his time, the incoming speaker shall only have his previously allocated time; or 3 ) Any other delegate not upon the Speaker's List, in which case the Director shall allow the incoming delegate to speak only for as long as the outgoing delegate had time remaining. A delegate may only yield to another delegate not on the Speakers' List if 1) He or she has time remaining; and 2 ) He or she did not take Points of Information  No more than two consecutive yields to other delegates will be accepted; the third yield will automatically go to the Director.  

 

SECTION J POINTS FROM THE FLOOR  

Rule 56 Available Points from the Floor  

There shall be five classes of points from the floor 1) Point of Order; 2 ) Points of Personal Privilege; 3 ) Points of Procedural Enquiry; 4 ) Point of Information; 5 ) National Right of Reply. The Director has the discretion to refuse frivolous or inappropriate points from the floor. No cross-floor debate shall be tolerated.  

Rule 57 Operation of Points of Order  

A delegate may call a Point of Order if another delegate or the Director is in breach of these rules of procedure. A Point of Order may interrupt the debate at any time. When a Point of Order is recognised, the delegate raising the Point is to explain, in less than thirty seconds, how procedure has not been followed. The Director shall then rule as to whether the Point is in or out of order.  

Rule 58 Operation of Points of Personal Privilege  

Points of Personal Privilege may be raised if a speaker is inaudible or any matter of the Committee environment is unsatisfactory. When a Point of Personal Privilege is recognised, the delegate raising the Point is to explain his or her concern in less than thirty seconds. Unless urgent, Points of Personal Privilege should be made in writing.  

Rule 59 Operation of Points of Procedural Enquiry  

A Point of Procedural Enquiry allows a delegate to clarify matters of procedure. Points of Procedural Enquiry may not interrupt speakers. When a Point of Procedural Enquiry is recognised, the delegate raising the Point is to outline, in less than thirty seconds, what element of procedure he or she wishes clarification on. Unless urgent, Points of Procedural Enquiry should be made in writing. Whether or not the Point is made orally, the Director shall respond with an oral declaration to all delegates. 

 Rule 60 Operation of Points of Information 

 Points of Information may be offered at the end of any speech arising from a non-procedural Speakers List. Points of Information may not be addressed to the Proposer or Seconder of a Draft Resolution, or the Proposer of an Amendment. Speakers may choose to accept or refuse Points of Information. If a speaker elects to take Points of Information, the Director will then invite delegates wishing to make such points to raise their placards, without speaking, and await recognition. The Director or delegate may limit the number of Points of Information at his or her discretion. All points of information are to be brief, relevant, and in the form of a question. 

 Rule 61 Operation of National Right of Reply  

If a delegate feels that his or her nation has been abused by a speaker, or that false statements have been made about his or her nation, he or she may stand, raise his or her placard, and await the completion of the speech. The Director may, if the speaker is extremely discourteous, interrupt the speech and request an immediate apology and/or issue a warning. At the completion of the speech, the Director will at his or her discretion recognise the standing delegate. The delegate may then give a speech of no more than one minute responding to the abuse or falsehood. The Director may then request that the abusive delegate apologise.  

 

SECTION K MOTIONS FROM THE FLOOR  

Rule 62 Definition of Procedural Motions 

 Procedural Motions allow delegates or the Director to exercise control over matters of procedure within the limits of these Rules of Procedure. Procedural motions may be made by any delegate or the Director. Procedural motions may not interrupt speakers. A delegate wishing to raise a procedural motion should stand, raise his or her placard and state the motion he or she wishes to raise. He or she may not speak to his or her motion. The Director may reject frivolous or inappropriate motions. A motion that has been recognised shall be put to the vote.  

Rule 63 Available Procedural Motions  

There are fourteen procedural motions available 1) Motion for a Moderated Caucus; 2 ) Motion to Extend the Moderated Caucus; 3 ) Motion to Return to Formal Debate; 4 ) Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus; 5 ) Motion to Introduce a Draft Resolution; 6 ) Motion to Introduce an Amendment; 7) Motion to Suspend Debate; 8) Motion to Resume Debate; 9 ) Motion to Close Debate; 10) Motion to Divide the Question; 11) Motion for Roll Call Vote; 12) Motion for Recount; 13) Motion for Procedural Debate; 14) Motion to Adjourn Session.  

Rule 64 Motion for a Moderated Caucus  

A Motion for a Moderated Caucus may be proposed throughout Formal Debate. Delegates proposing such a motion may wish to specify the proposed topic and duration of the Caucus. If a Motion for a Moderated Caucus passes, the Committee shall move directly to a Moderated Caucus.  

Rule 65 Motion to Extend the Moderated Caucus 

 A Motion to Extend the Moderated Caucus may be proposed during a Moderated Caucus. Delegates proposing such a motion may specify the duration to which he wishes the Moderated Caucus be extended. If a Motion to Extend the Moderated Caucus passes, the Caucus shall be extended.  

Rule 66 Motion to Return to Formal Debate 

 A Motion to Return to Formal Debate may be proposed during a Moderated Caucus. If such a motion is passed, the Committee shall return to Formal Debate.  

Rule 67 Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus 

 A Motion for Unmoderated Caucus may be proposed throughout Formal Debate. Delegates proposing such a motion may wish to specify the proposed duration of the Caucus. If a Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus passes, the Committee shall move directly to an Unmoderated Caucus.  

Rule 68 Motion to Introduce a Draft Resolution  

A Motion to Introduce Draft Resolution may be proposed during Formal Debate under the General Speakers' List. Only the Proposer of a Draft Resolution may raise this motion. If it motion passes, the proposed Draft Resolution shall be introduced under Rule 40.  

Rule 69 Motion to Introduce an Amendment 

A Motion to Introduce an Amendment may be proposed during Formal Debate under a Draft Resolution Speakers' List. Only the Proposer of an Amendment may raise this motion. If such a motion passes, the proposed Amendment shall be introduced under Rule 44.  

Rule 70 Motion to Suspend Debate A Motion to Suspend  

Debate may be proposed during Formal Debate under a Draft Resolution Speakers' List. If such a motion passes, the Draft Resolution shall be put aside, the Draft Resolution Speakers' List closed and suspended and the General Speakers' List resumed.  

Rule 71 Motion to Resume Debate  

A Motion to Resume Debate may be proposed during Formal Debate under the General Speakers' List when a Draft Resolution has previously been suspended. If such a motion passes, debate on the Draft Resolution shall be resumed. 

Rule 72 Motion to Close Debate  

A Motion to Close Debate may be proposed during Formal Debate under a Draft Resolution or Amendment Speakers' List. If proposed for a Draft Resolution, this Motion shall generate a Procedural Speakers' List. If this Motion passes, the Committee will move directly to a vote on the Draft Resolution or Amendment   

Rule 73 Motion to Divide the Question  

A Motion to Divide the Question may be proposed immediately prior to any vote on a Draft Resolution or Amendment. If this motion passes, the clauses shall be voted on individually by placard vote.  

Rule 74 Motion for Roll-Call Vote A Motion for Roll-Call  

Vote may be proposed immediately prior to a vote. The Director has a broad discretion to reject such motions in the interests of time. If this motion is passed, the vote shall be by roll-call. 

Rule 75 Motion for Recount  

A Motion for Recount may be proposed immediately after the announcement of the results of a vote. Such a motion shall not go to a vote, and shall be ruled upon at the Director's discretion. If the motion is passed by the Director, the vote shall be repeated.  

Rule 76 Motion for Procedural Debate 

 A Motion for Procedural Debate may be proposed after any procedural Motion has been proposed. The Director has discretion to entertain Motions for Procedural Debate. The passing of such a motion shall result in the creation of a Procedural Speakers' List.  

Rule 77 Motion to Adjourn Session 

 A Motion to Adjourn Session may be proposed throughout Formal Debate. The Director has a broad discretion to reject such motions. If such a Motion passes, the Committee shall be adjourned and the Director shall specify a time for the Committee to reconvene.  

Rule 78 Precedence of Motions and Points  

Motions and Points from the floor will be according the following precedence 1. Points of Personal Privilege (Rule 58); 2 . Point of Order (Rule 57); 3 . Points of Procedural Enquiry (Rule 59); 4 . National Right of Reply (Rule 61); 5 . Point of Information (Rule 60); 6 . Motion for Procedural Debate (Rule 76); 7. Motion to Suspend Debate (Rule 70); 8. Motion to Close Debate (Rule 72); 9 . Motion to Resume Debate (Rule 71); 10. Motion for a Moderated Caucus (Rule 64); 11. Motion to Extend the Moderated Caucus (Rule 65); 12. Motion to Return to Formal Debate (Rule 66); 13. Motion for an Unmoderated Caucus (Rule 67); 14. Motion to Introduce a Draft Resolution (Rule 68); 15. Motion to Introduce an Amendment (Rule 69); 16. Motion to Adjourn Session (Rule 77). During the voting procedure, the following Motions and Points shall be in order, and shall be according the following precedence 1. Points of Personal Privilege (Rule 58); 2 . Point of Order (Rule 57); 3 . Points of Procedural Enquiry (Rule 59); 4 . Motion for Procedural Debate (Rule 76); 5 . Motion to Divide the Question (Rule 73); 6 . Motion for Roll Call Vote (Rule 74); 7. Motion for Recount (Rule 75).  

 

SECTION L VOTING  

Rule 79 Classes of Vote  

There shall be two classes of vote procedural and substantive. All votes on Draft Resolutions shall be substantive. All votes on motions, amendments and any other matter shall be procedural. Delegates may elect to vote for, against or abstain on substantive votes, but may only vote for or against on procedural votes. All substantive votes shall be by roll-call. All procedural votes shall prima facie be by placard-vote.  

 Rule 80 Voting Rights  

Each Member State in the Committee shall have one vote. Observers do not have voting rights.  

Rule 81 Passing of Procedural Votes 

 All procedural votes require a simple majority of present and voting members to pass.  

Rule 82 Passing of Substantive Votes.  

Substantive votes shall be passed by simple majority of members present and voting. Members present and voting are those who vote either for or against but not those who abstain.  

Rule 83 Procedure of Placard Votes 

 In a placard vote, delegates vote by raising their placard only. 

 Rule 84 Procedure of Roll-Call Votes  

The Director shall read out the voting countries in alphabetical order. When a delegate's Member State is read out, he or she shall reply with “yes”, “no”, or “abstain”.  

Rule 85 Conduct during Voting.  

Note passing will be closed. No points of procedure or motions from the floor will be entertained during voting. A point of order may only be called if the Director has failed to oversee the vote in accordance with these rules  

Rule 86 Proxy Voting  

When a delegate is for whatever reason unable to remain in the Committee Chamber for a substantive vote, he or she may submit a proxy vote in writing to the Director.   

Rule 87 Right or Responsibility to Deliver Explanation of Voting 

 During any substantive vote, a delegate may request or be requested to explain his or her vote. Such requests may be made by the Director or a delegate at the completion of the vote but before the count of votes is announced. No delegate shall explain an abstention. Explanation may only be requested by another delegate if the vote goes against the known policy of the delegate's country or is at odds with his or her statements during debate. A delegate may, on voting, request the opportunity to explain by making a vote “yes, with rights” or “no, with rights”. The Director has discretion to refuse any request for explanation. Such explanation shall take place before the announcement of results, and shall be made in no more than thirty seconds.  

Rule 88 Equally divided votes.  

If a vote is equally divided and a simple majority is required, a recount shall be called. If the recount is equally divided 1) Procedural votes shall be taken as failed; 2 ) Substantive votes shall be suspended and debate resumed until the Director or a Delegate moves to return to the vote. If this subsequent vote is also equally divided, the proposal shall be taken as failed.
